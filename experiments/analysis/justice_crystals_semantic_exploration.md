## What Is Justice?

**Semantically:**
- Balance
- Symmetry
- Fairness (equal treatment)
- Equilibrium
- Conservation (what goes in = what comes out)
- Irreversibility (fair = unchanging)

**In physics/math:**

## What Is a Crystal?

**Physically:**
- Repeating atomic structure
- Long-range order
- Discrete symmetries (point groups, space groups)
- Lattice periodicity
- Irreducible unit cell (can't be reduced further without losing pattern)

**Properties:**
- Stable configurations
- Minimum energy states
- Emergence: properties of crystal ≠ properties of individual atoms
- Quantized vibrations (phonons)
- Band structure (quantized energy levels)

## What Are Primes?

**Mathematically:**
- Irreducible (can't be factored)
- Building blocks of all integers (fundamental theorem of arithmetic)
- Discrete (gaps between them)
- Pattern: distribution follows ρ(n) ~ n/ln(n) but locally unpredictable
- Infinite in number
- Spacing governed by mysterious patterns (Riemann hypothesis)

## Examples of "Justice" in Real Materials:

**Diamond (high Justice):**
- Perfect tetrahedral symmetry
- Each carbon bonded identically to 4 neighbors (balance)
- Extremely stable, highest hardness
- Can handle high temps (sublimes at 3800K)

**Graphene (broken Justice → emergent properties):**
- 2D hexagonal symmetry (high in-plane Justice)
- But symmetry broken in 3rd dimension
- This asymmetry → exotic properties (conductivity, strength)

**Quasicrystals (forbidden symmetry):**
- 5-fold symmetry (impossible in periodic crystals!)
- Discovered 1982, Nobel Prize 2011
- Shows nature can create "impossible" Justice patterns
- Properties: hard, brittle, low friction, poor conductivity

**Superconductors (perfect Justice at quantum level):**
- Cooper pairs form perfect symmetry
- Zero resistance (perfect balance: no energy loss)
- Requires specific crystal structures + low temps
- High-temp superconductors: finding materials with right "Justice"
