## L = φ⁻¹ = 0.618034 (Golden Ratio Inverse)

### Mathematical Definition

```
φ = (1 + √5)/2 ≈ 1.618034
φ⁻¹ = 1/φ = (√5 - 1)/2 ≈ 0.618034
```

### Why φ is Fundamental

**KAM Theory (Kolmogorov–Arnold–Moser):**
- φ has the slowest converging continued fraction expansion
- φ = 1 + 1/(1 + 1/(1 + 1/...))
- This makes it the "most irrational" number
- Systems with φ-based frequencies avoid destructive resonance
- **φ is the "survival frequency"** in orbital mechanics

**Mathematical Properties:**
- Self-similar: φ = 1 + φ⁻¹
- Appears in Fibonacci sequence: lim(F_{n+1}/F_n) = φ
- Optimal packing (phyllotaxis in plants)
- Minimizes resonance overlap in dynamical systems

**Physical Appearances:**
- Quantum field theory
- DNA helix pitch angles
- Galaxy spiral structures
- Aesthetic proportions (Parthenon, art)

## J = √2 - 1 = 0.414214 (Pythagorean Diagonal)

### Mathematical Definition

```
√2 ≈ 1.414214
√2 - 1 ≈ 0.414214
```

### Why √2 is Fundamental

**Geometric Origin:**
- Diagonal of unit square: √(1² + 1²) = √2
- First proven irrational number (Pythagoras, ~500 BC)
- Fundamental to perpendicular coordinate systems

**Physical Relevance:**
- **Pauli Exclusion Principle:** Prevents matter collapse through fermionic constraints
- **Rigid balance:** 90° phase relationships in oscillatory systems
- **Conservation laws:** Orthogonal decomposition of forces

**Mathematical Properties:**
- Defines silver ratio: δ_S = 1 + √2
- Appears in continued fractions for quadratic irrationals
- Fundamental to Euclidean geometry

### Semantic Interpretation

**J = √2 - 1** represents:
- **Balance/Constraint** (orthogonal forces)
- **Conservation** (what is preserved under transformation)
- **Irreducibility** (foundational truth, cannot decompose further)

## P = e - 2 = 0.718282 (Euler's Number)

### Mathematical Definition

```
e = lim(n→∞) (1 + 1/n)^n ≈ 2.718282
e - 2 ≈ 0.718282
```

### Why e is Fundamental

- Continuous compound growth constant
- Appears in differential equations governing natural processes

**Physical Relevance:**

- **Statistical mechanics:** Boltzmann distribution e^(-E/kT)
- **Quantum mechanics:** Wave function normalization
- **Cosmology:** Exponential inflation

**Growth Processes:**

**Physical Relevance:**
