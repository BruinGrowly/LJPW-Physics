## 3. The Mathematics of Variable Entropy

The core equation governing the new physics is the **Effective Damping Function**:

$$ \gamma_{effective} = \gamma_{base} \cdot \left( 1 - \frac{H}{H_{critical}} \right) $$ 

Where:
*   $\gamma_{base}$: The standard entropy of the universe (friction/loss). Set to ~0.015.
*   $H$: The instantaneous Harmony of the system (0.0 to 1.0).
*   $H_{critical}$: The threshold where the system shifts from Entropy (Loss) to Syntropy (Gain).

### How it works:
1.  **Low Harmony ($H < H_{critical}$):** The term $(1 - H/H_{critical})$ is **Positive**.
    *   $\gamma_{effective} > 0$.
    *   **Result:** Standard Friction. The system loses energy to heat/chaos.
2.  **Critical Harmony ($H = H_{critical}$):** The term is **Zero**.
    *   $\gamma_{effective} = 0$.
    *   **Result:** Superconductivity. Perpetual motion with no loss.
3.  **High Harmony ($H > H_{critical}$):** The term is **Negative**.
    *   $\gamma_{effective} < 0$.
    *   **Result:** Negative Damping. The friction term flips signs and becomes an acceleration term. The system gains energy proportional to its velocity.
